About Me

I am an environmental researcher specializing in environmental monitoring, metabarcoding, and molecular toxicology. My work focuses on understanding environmental pollutants and their effects on aquatic ecosystems, with expertise in biomaterials for environmental applications.

As a post-doctoral fellow at Chonnam National University's Fisheries Science Institute, I conduct research on environmental science with a focus on aquatic toxicology and sustainable solutions for environmental challenges.

With over 52 publications in international journals, my research has contributed to understanding the impacts of various pollutants, including microplastics, heavy metals, and emerging contaminants on marine and freshwater ecosystems.

Research Focus

My research investigates the intersection of environmental pollution, ecotoxicology, and sustainable solutions. Key areas include:

Environmental Monitoring & Toxicology

Investigating the impacts of pollutants on aquatic ecosystems, with a focus on heavy metals, microplastics, and emerging contaminants. Using advanced analytical techniques to monitor environmental quality and assess ecological risks.

Biofabricated Nanomaterials

Developing eco-friendly nanoparticles and assessing their potential in environmental remediation, antimicrobial applications, and as alternatives to conventional chemicals in various applications.

Molecular Responses to Environmental Stressors

Studying cellular and molecular responses to environmental contaminants in model organisms, including zebrafish and aquatic invertebrates, to understand toxicity mechanisms and develop biomarkers of exposure.

Metabarcoding & Environmental DNA

Applying molecular techniques to monitor biodiversity and ecosystem health, using DNA-based approaches to understand trophic interactions and community composition in aquatic environments.
